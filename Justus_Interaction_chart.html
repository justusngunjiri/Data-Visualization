#label
color_palette2 = alt.Scale(domain=['Renewable', 'Non-Renewable'],
                          range=['#FFD700', '#1E90FF'])
label = alt.selection_single(
    encodings=['x'],  #
    on='mouseover',   
    nearest=True,     
    empty='none'      
)

# Base chart with calculation
base = alt.Chart(energy_After_1960).mark_line().encode(
    alt.X('year:N', title='Year', 
          axis=alt.Axis(
              values=list(range(1960, 2026, 5)),
              labelFontWeight='bold',
              labelFontSize=14,  
              titleFontSize=12,  
              labelAngle=-45
          ), scale=alt.Scale(zero=False)
    ),
    alt.Y('sum(consumption):Q', title='Total Consumption (TWh)',
          axis=alt.Axis(
              labelFontWeight='bold',
              values=list(range(0, 240000, 50000)),
              labelFontSize=14
          )),
    color=alt.Color('energy_type:N', title='Energy Type', scale=color_palette2),
).transform_fold(
    fold=['coal_consumption', 'solar_consumption', 'gas_consumption', 'hydro_consumption', 'wind_consumption', 'oil_consumption'],
    as_=['energy_type', 'consumption']
).transform_calculate(
    energy_type='datum.energy_type == "coal_consumption" || datum.energy_type == "gas_consumption" || datum.energy_type == "oil_consumption" ? "Non-Renewable" : "Renewable"'
)

# Vertical line
vertical_line = alt.Chart().mark_rule(color='gray').encode(
    x='year:N'
).transform_filter(label)

interactive_chart = alt.layer(
    base,  
    vertical_line,  
    base.mark_circle().encode(
        opacity=alt.condition(label, alt.value(1), alt.value(0))   
    ).add_selection(label),
    base.mark_text(align='left', dx=5, dy=-5, stroke='white', strokeWidth=2).encode(
        text='sum(consumption):Q'   
    ).transform_filter(label),
    base.mark_text(align='left', dx=5, dy=-5).encode(
        text='sum(consumption):Q'  
    ).transform_filter(label),
).properties(
    width=800,
    height=400,
    data=energy_After_1960,
    title='Energy Consumption Over the Years (1960 onwards) - On-Demand Interaction'
)

The encoding I used in this energy consumption visualization improves the effectiveness in conveying the data, and the addition of on-demand interaction further enhances its utility. Because I was interested on both single point and trend over time, selection of a single point for on-demand interaction empowers the user to dynamically explore the data, offering a more engaging and informative experience. Allowing users to retrieve the specific information about energy consumption by simply hovering over data points, the visualization becomes more accessible. The colors were also carefully selected to ensure accessibility for people with color vision deficiency. The vertical black line serves as a guide and the conditional opacity encoding for selected data points, offers a clear visual indication of the data of interest, making it easier for one to distinguish and focus on relevant trend line. Combination of the chosen encodings and on-demand interaction thus improve the chart's accessibility and usability, making it a powerful in the data exploration and analysis of the energy data.
